Tips

The treatment of Diabetes Mellitus (DM) is based on: proper nutrition and proper diabetes education,
hypoglycemic drugs (exclusively in type 2 DM) and insulin.
Food is essential in the care and management of diabetes, constituting one of the fundamental pillars for good diabetes control.
For the proper implementation of the diet, a series of dietary recommendations have been written that the person with diabetes should know and carry out.
The objectives of the diet for the diabetic population are:

  • Maintain glycemia within appropriate limits, modifying food intake with medication and physical activity.
  • Normalize the lipid profile and maintain good blood pressure control.
  • Keep the weight within normal limits.

  • Be balanced and complete.
  • Provide the calories needed to maintain or reduce weight and, in children and adolescents, allow for proper growth and development.
  • Increase caloric needs in pregnancy and lactation.
  • Prevent and help treat acute complications, especially hypoglycemia, and chronic complications.

In the population of people with DM an intake is recommended:

  • Varied, that allows compliance and that provides us with all the macro and micronutrients necessary for the proper functioning of our body.
  • Protein intake should constitute around 10-20% of total daily calories. In those with diabetic neuropathy the consumption of Recommended protein is lower, around 10%.
  • The fat contribution must be less than 10% in saturated fats and ≤10% in polyunsaturated fats. The rest of the fats should be monounsaturated. Cholesterol intake should be less than 300 mg / day.
  • Carbohydrates: simple sugars should be restricted and those ingested should be complex. Sweeteners can be used, the consumption of fiber, cereals and legumes must be increased
