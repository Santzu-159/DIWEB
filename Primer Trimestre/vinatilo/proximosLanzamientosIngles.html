The Rolling Stones will launch a vinyl collection





The British band The Rolling Stones will publish a compilation of 15 vinyls with their greatest hits on June 15, according to Universal label.
                            Under the name The Studio Albums Vinyl Collection 1971-2016, the Rolling Stones will review their trajectory over more than 40 years in an edition box                             limited that will include 15 studio albums.

                            They are among them from the classic Sticky Fingers, with the cover of the zipper designed by artist Andy Warhol,                             until the last Blue & Lonesome, launched in 2016, going through jewels like It's Only Rock'n'Roll and Voodo Lounge, from 1974 and 1994, respectively.                             

                            The discs have been remastered to                             from original tapes to get the best possible sound in the mythical London studios of Abbey Road, where, for example, the Beatles recorded their latest album.
                            Each album is pressed in 180 gram black vinyl and includes a download card for digital listening, as well as a certificate of authenticity.                             

                            The included discs are the following: Sticky Fingers (1971); Exile On Main St (1972); Goats Head Soup (1973); It's Only Rock'n'Roll (1974); Black And Blue (1976);                             Some Girls (1978); Emotional Rescue (1980); Tattoo You (1981); Undercover (1983); Dirty Work (1986); Steel Wheels (1989); Voodoo Lounge (1994); Bridges To Babylon (1997);                             To Bigger Bang (2005) and Blue & Lonesome (2016). This spring, the Rolling Stones will turn for the first time in the UK since 2006, with performances scheduled for the 17th                             May in Dublin; for May 22 in London; on June 5, in Manchester; on June 9, in Edinburgh; 15, in Cardiff (Wales), and 19 at Twickenham Cricket Stadium,                             in London.

A new vinyl box collects records and discontinued Bruce Springsteen



A new compilation box will allow to dispose as of May 18 of the LP that Bruce Springsteen published between 1987 and 1996 and have been discontinued for some time, in a limited edition and remastered for the first time on vinyl.



This is what Columbia Records has advanced today, in conjunction with Legacy Recordings, which thanks to Bruce Springsteen: The Album Collection Vol. 2, 1987-1996                                 it will again circulate albums like Tunnel of love, Human Touch, Lucky Town and The Ghost of Tom Joad.

                                                                 The first two will appear expanded to the double LP format to "maximize audio quality", which has been taken care of by engineer Bob Ludwig                                 and the usual employee of the Boss, the engineer Toby Scott.

                                                                 In addition, of the four studio albums published in those years, the compilation will include a 12-inch EP special                                 1988 live Chimes of Freedom, the 1993 Springsteen LP double MTV Plugged and the first vinyl release of the 1996 EP Blood Brothers.

                                                                  Other releases

                                                                 This collection will hit the market after the launch of 2014 with the title Bruce Springsteen: The Album Collection Vol. 1, 1973-1984,                                 which included remastered editions of the first seven studio albums of the New Jersey musician. The ten albums that make up this collection                                 they are presented in reproductions of the original covers, accompanied by a book of 60 pages with little known photos, objects of interest and                                 original news from that period.
